Superimposed Graph

KG1 as the skeleton. KG2 as the truth test.

This is the actual direction you described: one shared node-and-edge schema, with the gold-standard graph always visible and each model overlaid on top. What the model reproduces becomes solid. What it misses remains exposed. What it gets wrong is impossible to hide.

Why this works

KG1 never disappears. The reader always sees the clinical reference structure.
Recovered edges sit on the same geometry. No mental remapping between panels is needed.
Missing and wrong edges are explicit. The graph itself becomes the evaluation surface.

Edge language

KG1 reference edge Recovered correctly Not replicated Wrong or spurious Weak / partial

Reading rule

Read the faint blue skeleton first. Then look for where the model paints over it in green, leaves it untouched in grey, or draws contradictory red paths. Under-recovery stays visible on the same graph instead of being hidden in a separate metric panel.
